Notline Miami

NickDecember 21, 2017March 22, 2018

Notline Miami was the final project for two of my classes this semester. My goal was to combine the requirements of my UI scripting class and my gameplay scripting class to make a more complete game. Ultimately I wanted to create something that heavily mimicked the style of Hotline Miami (hence the name).

For the gameplay, I wanted a few things. Namely guns, enemies, and the objective which is to just kill everyone. The gun system was really fun to make and ended up being quite complex. I am very happy with how it turned out. That being said I wish I had time to implement melee weapons. The enemy AI was quite difficult to make, as all AI is. It is far from perfect, and this is probably the most choppy part of the project, but at the end of the day, AI is not my focus as a designer. The guns, enemy, and player come together to create a gameplay experience that is more like Hotline Miami than it is not, and I am happy about this. Given the period I had to work on this (~2 weeks) I am quite happy with the end result.

The UI side of this is probably what I enjoyed the most. On the functionality side of things, I enjoy scripting for UI more interesting and fun than other focuses (ahem… AI). Something great that I have taken away is a handful of scripts that I wrote that make certain generic UI tasks essentially drag and drop, no unique code required. These scripts will be a great foundation for the UI in my future projects. On the visual side of things for the UI, I really wanted to nail the aesthetic of the game. If it were up to me, I would have made it as close to 1:1 as possible, but I had certain grading requirements that forced me to deviate from the source. A great example of this would be the process of respawning. In the Hotline Miami it simply tells you to press ‘R’ to respawn, but I had to have a dedicated screen with several other buttons on this ‘Game Over’ screen. Overall I think that I nailed the style of UI in Hotline Miami. The funny thing is that I didn’t need to do this at all, as visuals were not a grading point of the assignment, but I figured if I was going to do it at all, I may as well do it right. While I got no extra points for the visual aspects of the project, I definitely learned a lot of things in the process, namely certain UI design principles, the importance of animations, motion, and use of color. I am not an artist, but these skills are important in many aspects of design.

Ultimately, I am very happy with this project. It is by far the most complicated code I have written to this point, and in the process, I learned a lot. Unfortunately, aside from the UI, design became something of an afterthought, as I became so absorbed into the technical side of things. Because of this, I cannot see this becoming a portfolio piece, but it will have a special place with this post, as well as in my heart. I have linked the playable build and Github page below!
