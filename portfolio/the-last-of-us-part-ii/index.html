The Last of Us Part II

Category Portfolio, Quality Assurance

Introduction

For my first job out of college, I was fortunate enough to get a position in the QA department at Naughty Dog working on The Last of Us Part II. I joined the project in the last year of production, so I really had to hit the ground running. Overall working on this game was an amazing opportunity, I was able to work with nearly every department in the studio. It was an amazing experience and I learned so much from the talented people I worked with every day.

My Roles

Level Area Contact

A few weeks after getting hired I was assigned to be a point of contact for two of the larger levels in the game, along with a partner. My responsibilities encompassed making sure the levels were stable with no major bugs, completing test cases to catch large amounts of smaller bugs, as well as completing various passes & other routine checks throughout the week. Additionally I was responsible for responding to any requests/callouts from devs working on the level. This included things such as tweaking nav mesh, tagging object surfaces, or simply just logging a bug someone caught while playing.

This was an amazing opportunity to work directly with the design team, as well as the people from other departments working on these levels. It was extremely inspiring and educational to get to see some of the best of the best in action.

Particles Point of Contact

In addition to being responsible for two levels, at the start of 2020 I was made the point of contact for all particles/vfx related issues. My responsibilities included handling all incoming requests from the particles team, as well as performing routine passes on features as they were implemented. A lot of the hard work had already been done by the previous point of contact, but there was still a lot of work left to do!

This was also an amazing opportunity to work with the particles department, another team of incredibly talented individuals. This area of testing seemed to be something many in QA weren’t as familiar or comfortable with, so it was empowering to become a source of knowledge and help for my coworkers coming across particle issues in their levels.
